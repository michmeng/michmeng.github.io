Basic Lesson on Elementary Epigenomics

In collaboration with two other University of California, San Diego students, I developed an elementary lesson on epigenomics.

In examining the diversity of life, we're often directed to the sequence of an organism's DNA as the source of variation. However, in this paper, we wish to look beyond the genome. Instead, we introduce the topic of epigenomics, the analysis of gene expression that is not attributable to mutations to the DNA sequence of a genome. Rather, epigenetic expression alteration is caused by biochemical interactions with various proteins and other compounds. Find the entire lesson here!

Replication Origin

Replication origins not only give us insight into how cells replicate, but also help us in innovating gene therapies by studying how to develop viral vectors to encode specific therapeutic proteins within a patient's cells. To find these locations it is important to find the most frequent "words" within a sequence, but to also account for its "neighbors" and reverse compliments. Another commonly used method is to find the position of minimum skew on the genome.
